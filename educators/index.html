THE INSTRUCTOR PROBLEM

The final answer hides the learning process.

Students struggle, seek help, revise, and build understanding before they submit. Most of that process is invisible to the instructor.

  • AI use happens outside the course context.
  • Struggle and revision remain hidden.
  • Useful feedback arrives after the learning moment.

THE MYTA WORKFLOW

Set the context. Guide the work. See the learning.

  1. 1

    Create the assignment

    Add course materials, define the objective, and choose how AI may support students.

  2. 2

    Students work with guided AI

    Students receive course-grounded questions, hints, references, and checks while completing the assignment.

  3. 3

    Review learning signals

    See class and student patterns in confusion, help seeking, revision, and understanding.

INSTRUCTOR FEATURES

Control the experience. Understand the process.

Course context

Bring slides, notes, readings, examples, and assignment directions into one bounded context.

AI guidance rules

Choose what support is allowed, how the AI should respond, and when understanding should be checked.

Guided student reasoning

Keep help focused on questions, hints, explanations, course references, and revision.

Instructor insight

Surface where students struggled, what support they used, and what may need attention.
